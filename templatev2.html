Our Approach

We use service design thinking to resolve physical, spatial and operational challenges. Service design involves the application of human centric approach based on designthinking to solve complex physical, spatial and operational problems and to de-risk digital innovation and product development.

Our methodology is uniquely applied to each challenge, though broadly falls into four main phases.

Discovery, Deﬁne, Design and Deliver..

Next

Transformational Change

For complex operational or organizational transformation projects, we take a human-centred approach to understand and map the larger picture. Our discovery phase focuses on understanding how customers or users are actually impacted by your services, products and organizational behaviors.

We also interview and analyse internal staﬀ, processes, systems and even spaces that people work in to understand how these are driving issues both within the organization and externally with customers or users. The design and delivery phases focus on identifying and creating new processes, organizational designs, policies and the tools and services required to improve how your teams work and the overall customer experience.

Next

Product Innovation

The challenge with more innovation and product development initiatives is that they are often technologyled programmes of work, which are retroﬁtted to meet assumed customer needs. We have adapted our methodology to ﬁt within a lean and iterative development framework.

Our unique iterative service design approach runs a pace ahead of a lean or agile UX process to constantly identify and reﬁne changing customer needs. We then work hand in hand with UX, development and user testing to ensure that each iteration or phase of new products continue to meet real customer needs and deliver real customer value, rather than just test well as a UI design pattern.

Our Process

We use primary research to understand what customers, citizens, staﬀ and others actually do, think, feel, need and experience. Our research methods are often intensive and rigorously documented. We are often run collaborative sessions with members of the client’s organisation to aggregate internal knowledge.

Research is analysed and turned into ease to understand artefacts such as journey maps, which illustrate what people actually do and how it aﬀects the overall experience. The deﬁne stage is useful for understanding people’s requirements and real issues, and for spotting initial opportunities.

Solutions are then deﬁned, often collaboratively with our clients and customers and brought to life in blueprints, storyboards, future state journeys and other artefacts.

The delivery phase can be a single output to an iterative, “test – learn – reﬁne – launch – repeat” process that can continue as long as required. Outputs can range anything from new processes and staﬀ policies, to digital, service and spatial prototypes, which we then test.
