We Are Hominal

We are a design consultancy focused around using human-centred approaches to solve complex operational challenges, shape new customer experiences and define new products and services.

We work across numerous sectors delivering change and improvements to processes and services both customer facing and behind the scenes. We believe in a combination of the power of engaged people and customers, and ensuring that the enabling technology is so seamlessly integrated in the way people work, that it becomes almost invisible.

Our Approach

We use service design thinking to resolve physical, spatial and operational challenges. Service design involves the application of human centric approach based on designthinking to solve complex physical, spatial and operational problems and to de-risk digital innovation and product development.

Our methodology is uniquely applied to each challenge, though broadly falls into four main phases.

Discovery, Deﬁne, Design and Deliver..

Next

Transformational Change

When approaching complex operational or organizational transformation projects, we take a human-centred approach. We use behavioural and process research to understand and map the larger picture. Our discovery phase focuses on understanding how your users are impacted by your services, products and organizational behaviors.

We also interview and analyse internal staﬀ, processes, systems and the physical spaces that people work in to understand how these are affecting performance both within the organization and externally with customers or users. The design and delivery phases focus on identifying and creating new processes, organizational designs, policies and tools. We use an iterative approach to measuring the impact and effectiveness of these improvements across a set of financial, structural and experiential criteria that are relevant to our clients’ business outcomes.

Product Innovation

The challenge with many innovation and product development initiatives is that they are often technology led programmes of work, which are retroﬁtted to meet assumed customer needs. We believe that change begins with people, and understanding how they behave, interact and work. Technology should underpin the core capabilities of your organisation, not dictate them, and Hominal use their experience of working in real-world programme delivery environments to ensure tangible results are delivered.

Our unique iterative service design approach runs alongside and ahead of a Lean or Agile UX and development process to constantly identify and reﬁne changing customer needs. We then work hand in hand with internal or supplier teams to ensure that each iteration or phase of new products continue to meet business and customer requirements and deliver real value at launch and long into the future.

Our Process

We use primary research to understand what customers, citizens, staﬀ and others actually do, think, feel, need and experience. Our research methods are often intensive and rigorously documented. We are often run collaborative sessions with members of the client’s organisation to aggregate internal knowledge.

Research is analysed and turned into ease to understand artefacts such as journey maps, which illustrate what people actually do and how it aﬀects the overall experience. The deﬁne stage is useful for understanding people’s requirements and real issues, and for spotting initial opportunities.

Solutions are then deﬁned, often collaboratively with our clients and customers and brought to life in blueprints, storyboards, future state journeys and other artefacts.

The delivery phase can be a single output to an iterative, ‘test – learn – reﬁne – launch – iterate’ process that can continue and deliver improvement through the life of the product or service. Outputs can range anything from new processes and staﬀ policies, to digital, service and spatial prototypes, which we then test and implement.

Meet the founders

Hominal is founded by Dom Graveson and Douglas M. Smith.

Dom Graveson

Dom has a background of leading and driving digital change across a wide range of sectors from finance to broadcast and media; publishing to charity and government. He is passionate about the importance of the human and cultural aspects of technology assisted change in organisations and society, and believes that behavioural research makes up an important precursor to any successful strategy. He is a regular speaker and chair at conferences across Europe and has a particular interest in how digital technology is transforming education and organisational collaboration and change.

He has worked on both digital strategy and change programme delivery and evaluation with organisations across the world including BSkyB, Carlton TV, Catlin, Aviva, Action Aid, World Bank, Slovenian Education Ministry, Bourne Leisure, and SABMiller.

Douglas M. Smith

Douglas comes from EY-Seren, where, as Head of Experience Design, he led Service and UX Design teams across a number of transformational projects within the finance, travel & leisure, telecom, healthcare and government sectors. He has a 25+ year career spanning digital and mobile strategy, and experience and service design.

His expertise has been trusted by many of the world’s and Britain’s top companies; ranging from Microsoft, VW, Cigna, Lexus & Unilever; to Lloyds, RBS, Topman, Virgin, Saga, Dixon, Bourne Leisure, and others.
