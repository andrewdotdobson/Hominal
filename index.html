Our Approach

We use service design thinking to resolve physical, spatial and operational challenges. Service design involves the application of human centric approach based on design thinking to solve complex physical, spatial and operational problems and to de-risk digital innovation and product development.

Our methodology is uniquely applied to each challenge, though broadly falls into four main phases.

Discovery, Deﬁne, Design and Deliver..

Next

Transformational Change

When approaching complex operational or organizational transformation projects, we take a human-centred approach. We use behavioural and process research to understand and map the larger picture. Our discovery phase focuses on understanding how your users are impacted by your services, products and organizational behaviours.

We also interview and analyse internal staff, processes, systems and the physical spaces that people work in in order to understand how these affect performance both within the organization and externally with customers or users. The design and delivery phases focus on identifying and creating new processes, organizational designs, policies and tools. We use an iterative approach to measure the impact and effectiveness of these improvements across a set of financial, structural and experiential criteria that are relevant to our clients’ specific business outcomes.

Product Innovation

The challenge with many innovation and product development initiatives is that they are often technology-led programmes of work, which are retroﬁtted to meet assumed customer needs. We believe that change begins with people and with understanding how they behave, interact and work. Technology should enable and mobilize the core capabilities of your organisation, not define them, and Hominal use their experience of working in real-world programme delivery environments to ensure tangible results are delivered.

Our unique iterative service design approach runs alongside and ahead of a Lean or Agile UX and development process to constantly identify and reﬁne changing customer needs. We then work hand-in-hand with internal or supplier teams to ensure that each iteration or phase of new products continue to meet business and customer requirements and to deliver real value both at launch and long into the future.

Our Process

We use primary research to understand what customers, citizens, staff and others actually do, think, feel, need and experience. Our research methods are often intensive and rigorously documented. We often run collaborative sessions with members of the client’s organisation to aggregate internal knowledge.

Research is analysed and turned into easy-to-understand artefacts, such as journey maps, which illustrate what people actually do and how it affects the overall experience. The deﬁne stage is useful for understanding people’s requirements and real issues, and for spotting initial opportunities for immediate impact as well as longer-term transformation.

Solutions are then deﬁned, often collaboratively with our clients and customers, and brought to life in blueprints, storyboards, future state journeys and other artefacts. These solutions are then rigorously tested in various prototype stages to ensure we understand the most appropriate way to implement them at scale.

The delivery phase can be a single output to an agile, ‘test > learn > reﬁne > launch > iterate’ process that can continue and deliver improvement through the life of the product or service. Outputs can range from new processes and staff policies, to digital, service and spatial prototypes, which we then partner with you to test and implement.
